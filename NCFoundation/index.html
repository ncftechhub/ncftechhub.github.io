Our Story

Namuwongo Community Foundation, Non-Profit Organization

Namuwongo Community Foundation(NCF) is a Ugandan Charity Organsation dedicated to transforming lives through education, empowerment and community development.
We support vulnerable children, struggling mothers and disadvantaged youths by providing free education, vocational/DIT training and economic opportunities.
Rooted in Namuwongo, NCF strives to create a future where everyone has the opportunity to thrive.
It was established in February 2017 officially incorporated on 22nd May 2018.

Our Mission

To empower individuals and communities to realize their full potentials through education.

Christine Kasapuuli Atwooki

Founder of NCF

Behind the transformative work of the Namuwongo Community Foundation stands a woman whose life journey is a testament of resilience, compassion and purpose. Her name is Christine Kasapuuli Atwooki, a name now synonymous with hope in the heart of Namuwongo slums residents. Raised in a home rooted in generosity, Christine was deeply shaped by her parents' love and giving values that would later form the foundation of her life's mission. But everything changed when she lost both parents at a tender age of 13. Suddenly, the warmth of home was replaced by the harsh reality of surviva. She and her siblings struggled to stay in school, often unsure where the next term's schhol fees would comne from. Yet even in the darkest hour, kindness found her. Remarkable individuals, some strangers, some friends, reached out lifting her from despair and giving her a chance to dream again. Their generosity became the wind beneath her wings, propelling her towards an extra ordinary academic and professional journey.

Today Christine holds a bachelors degree in Procurement and Supply Management from Kampala International University, a bachelors degree in law from Uganda Christian University and two master's degrees one in Human Resource Management and the other in Business Administration both from Uganda Management Institute. Her achievments are more than academic milestones they are symbols of resilience, gratitude and power of second chance But she did not stop there. Driven by a desire to ensure no child in Namuwongo suffers as she once did, Christine founded the Namuwongo Community Foundation, a safe haven and a launching pad for countless dreams. Thanks to her vision NCF has produced .Doctors healing the underserved .Lawyers standing up for the voiceless .Nurses caring with heart .Teachers shaping future generations .Engineers designing brighter solutions

Ssozi Ashraf

Head of Media at NCF

Sozi Ashraf first joined the Namuwongo Community foundation as a volunteer, driven by passion to empower children through the arts. His initial role was to train children in a theatrical performance showcased during the Ewafe Jenva Fundraising event an initiative aimed at saving the Namuwongo Community Medical Service Center from closure due to lack of funds.Impressed by his creativity, dedication and outstanding leadership, Sozi was soon appointed as head of media. In his role, he runs all the NCF's social media platforms while continuing to mentor children through arts, media and digital training.

His impact has been nothing short of transformative. Sozi has spearheaded and planned numerous fundraising campaigns, including Edition 1 and 2 of Mutuwe Naffe Tuliwa Abalala ("Give us, we shall also give others"), a vibrant talent show and cutural showcase that have taken NVF children beyond their community to perform at school events and most reently, to deliver a breathtaking traditional dance performance at Quonnect Market Day.

Key Objectives for Our Charity:

  • Handles media activities of NCF
  • Digital marketing and graphic design
  • Safe digital consumption
  • Mentoring sessions
